## 📊 Key Financial Terms Explained

### EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, Amortization)
Operating profit before finance costs and accounting depreciation. Shows true operational profitability.
- **Higher is better** because it's independent of capital structure (debt levels) and accounting policies.
- **EBITDA Margin** = EBITDA ÷ Revenue. Tata Motors: 13.2% in FY26.

### ROCE (Return on Capital Employed)
How much profit is generated from every rupee of capital (equity + debt) invested in the business.
- **Formula:** EBIT ÷ (Equity + Net Debt) or NOPAT ÷ Invested Capital
- **Benchmark:** >15% = good; >20% = excellent; <10% = weak
- **Tata Motors:** 72% in FY26 = exceptional (far exceeds cost of capital)

### Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Cash generated from operations minus capital expenditure. **Most important metric for value investors.**
- **Formula:** Operating Cash Flow − Capex
- **Why it matters:** Shows cash available to shareholders after reinvestment; can't be manipulated by accounting
- **Tata Motors FY26:** ₹12,400 Cr = ~12% of revenue (healthy conversion)

### Debt/Equity Ratio
Total debt divided by shareholders' equity. Measures financial leverage.
- **<1.0** = Safe (company has more equity than debt)
- **1.0 – 2.0** = Moderate (balanced capital structure)
- **>2.0** = Leveraged (high financial risk)
- **Tata Motors:** Net cash positive (no debt risk)

### Interest Coverage Ratio
Earnings available to pay interest on debt. EBIT ÷ Interest Expense.
- **>3.0** = Healthy (can pay interest 3+ times over)
- **1.5 – 3.0** = Watch (monitor carefully)
- **<1.5** = Risk (difficulty servicing debt)
- **Tata Motors:** Strong profitability; no interest coverage concerns

### Price-to-Earnings (PE) Ratio
Stock price divided by annual earnings per share (EPS).
- **Shows:** How much investors are willing to pay per rupee of earnings
- **Higher PE** = Market expects faster growth OR market is overvaluing
- **Lower PE** = Market expects slower growth OR stock is cheap
- **Tata Motors:** 46.28x = premium (suggests high growth expectations priced in)

### Price-to-Book (PB) Ratio
Stock price divided by book value per share (assets − liabilities ÷ shares).
- **PB > 1** = Trading above book value (market values the business highly)
- **PB < 1** = Trading below book value (rare; indicates distress or deep value)
- **Tata Motors:** 36.38x = extremely high (priced for perfection)
